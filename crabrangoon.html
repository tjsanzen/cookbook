Crab Rangoons

Panda Express has these rangoons on their menu but they only use cream cheese. I love the addition of crab. It makes the fried wontons taste so much more refreshing and I could barely even taste seafood. You can use imitation crab meat or the real deal depending on your own preference. Just serve with sweet and sour sauce or a sweet chili sauce and there you have it!

90 Minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 Cups Crab Meat
  • 3/4 Cup Cream Cheese
  • 1 Tablespoon Worcestershire Sauce
  • 1/2 Teaspoon Garlic Powder
  • 1/2 Teaspoon Salt
  • 1/2 Teaspoon Black Pepper
  • 1 Egg
  • Wonton Wrappers
  • Green Onions
  • Vegetable Oil for Frying
  • Chili Sauce for Dipping
  1. In a large bowl, mix together crab meat, cream cheese, Worcestershire sauce, garlic powder, salt, and black pepper. If you'd like, you can also add chopped green onions.

  2. In a small bowl, beat the egg with a splash of water.

  3. On your working surface, lay a wonton wrapper. Place about a tablespoon or less of crab filling in the center. Brush the whole border with egg wash. Bring the opposite ends together and pinch to seal tight. Make sure to push any excess air out to prevent them from popping when frying. Repeat until all the crab filling is done.

  4. In a pot, add oil over medium-high heat and let it reach frying temperature (350Â°F). Drop the wontons in, making sure not to overcrowd them. Fry in batches. Make sure to press them down so that they fry evenly. Once they are golden brown, take off the oil and transfer to plate with a paper towel to drain any excess oil.

  5. Serve with sweet chili sauce or sweet and sour sauce and enjoy!
