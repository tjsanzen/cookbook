In-N-Out Burger

An American classic, this In-N-Out style burger is a must have for those hot summer days with friends!

90 Minutes
8 Servings

Ingredients

  • 1/3 Cup Mayonnaise
  • 4 Tablespoons Pickle Relish, drained
  • 2-3 Tablespoons Ketchup
  • 1 Teaspoon Sugar
  • 1 Tablespoon White Vinegar
  • 1 Large Onion, diced
  • 1/4 Cup Yellow Mustard
  • 8 Slices American Cheese
  • 1 Sliced Onion, grilled
  • 1/2 Tablespoon Vegetable Oil
  • 1/4 Teaspoon Salt
  • 4 Tablespoons Water
  • 1.5 to 2 pounds Ground Beef
  • 4 Tablespoons Butter
  • 1-2 Tablespoons Vegetable Oil
  • 8 Hamburger Buns
  • 4 Sliced Tomatos 
  • Iceberg Lettuce
  1. Make the Spread: In a small bowl, mix together the mayonnaise, relish, ketchup, sugar, and vinegar. It will be a pink color. Set aside. 

  2. Prepare Toppings: Slice tomatoes, and tear off pieces of lettuce.

  3. Caramelize Onions: Dice 1 large onion finely. In a saute pan over low heat, add 1/2 tablespoon vegetable oil, diced onions, and 1/4 teaspoon salt. Continuously stir, making sure to scrape the pan, for about 12 minutes until it starts to get golden brown. Then, turn up the heat to medium. Add 1 tablespoon of water to the onions and stir until it evaporates. Repeat this process with 3 tablespoons of water, one at t a time. 

  4. Prepare the Patties: Weigh out 8 portions of 3 to 4 oz of ground beef or eye it out carefully. Gently flatten the patty into a circle to desired thin or thickness, making sure to not mix or mold too much. Sprinkle with salt and pepper on both sides. 


  5. Toast the Buns: Melt 2 tablespoons of butter. Brush the insides of both buns with the butter and place face down on a pan over medium heat. Take them off the pan when a nice golden brown develops around the edges. 


  6. Cook the Patties: Over medium-high heat in a skillet or pan, add 1-2 tablespoons butter and 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil. Add the patties in and if doing animal style, add half a tablespoon or more to taste of mustard on the raw side and spread evenly. It will take about 2-3 minutes for the patty to get a nice brown crust. Flip over and add a slice of American cheese on top. Let cook for about 1 more minute before taking off heat. 


  7. Assemble: Add spread to the bottom of the bun. Then add the tomatoes, lettuce, patty and cheese, onions, and a final piece of cheese before the top of the bun. Seal the burger and enjoy!
