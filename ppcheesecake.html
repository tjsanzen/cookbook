Pumpkin Pie Cheesecake Bars

Not your typical cheesecake bars! Instead of pumpkin-spiced cheesecake, we're combining our two favorite desserts here!


60 Minutes
8 Servings

Ingredients

  • 18 Honey Graham Crackers
  • 8 Tablespoons Unsalted Butter, melted
  • 1/4 Cup Light Brown Sugar
  • 1/8 Teaspoon Salt
  • 2 Packages Cream Cheese, room temperature
  • 1 Cup Granulated Sugar
  • 1/2 Tablespoon Corn Starch
  • 1 Tablespoon All Purpose Flour
  • 1/4 Cup Sour Cream
  • 3 Large Eggs, room temperature
  • 1 Tablespoon Vanilla Extract
  • 1 Can Pumpkin Puree, 15 oz
  • 1 Can Condensed Milk, 14 oz
  • 2 Eggs, room temperature
  • 1 Teaspoon Ground Cinnamon
  • 1/2 Teaspoon Ground Ginger
  • 1/2 Teaspoon Ground Nutmeg
  • 1/2 Teaspoon Salt
  1. Preheat oven to 350F. Butter and line a 13x9 inch pan with parchment paper. Set aside.

  2. Make the Crust: Add the graham crackers into a food processor until they have completely crumbled (sand texture). In a medium bowl, combine graham crackers, melted butter, light brown sugar, and salt. Press into the bottom of the pan tightly and create a smooth crust layer. Bake for 8-10 minutes. Remove from the oven and allow to cool. 

  3. Make the Cheesecake: Using the paddle attachment of a stand mixer, beat cream cheese, granulated sugar, cornstarch, and flour together until smooth. Do not overbeat. Scrape the sides of the bowl, then add the sour cream, eggs, vanilla extract, and salt. Beat on low speed just until you have a smooth batter. Stop right away and set aside. 


  4. Make the Pumpkin Pie: Add the pumpkin puree, condensed milk, eggs, cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, and salt into a bowl. Whisk all together until combined. 


  5. Pour the cheesecake batter over the cooled crust. Then pour the pumpkin pie batter on top of the cheesecake batter, making sure to pour it across the surface. Also make sure to pour it in gently or very close to the pan. 

  6. Bake for 45 minutes. The center will still be a bit jiggly but the edges should be firm. Allow to cool to room temperature, then refrigerate overnight.

  7. Take the cheesecake out of the fridge. Remove from the pan by lifting the parchment paper. Cut into squares wiping the knife clean between slices.
